Resources

While we process your complaint, you may find some of these resources helpful.

Legal advice and representation in civil cases for low-income Utahns
Utah Legal Services is a nonprofit law office. They provide free legal help to low-income Utahns in non-criminal cases. Their website also has more information for consumers.

Various businesses and professionals are required to license and/or register with the Utah Department of Commerce. You can check this list to see which division of the Department a certain type of business or professional is supposed to license and/or register with.

Search for registered entities with the Division of Consumer Protection
Most businesses must register with the state Division of Consumer Protection. If you believe an individual or group may have pretended to be a real business when they are not, you can use this search to see if they have registered.


Search for licensees with the Division of Securities
Individuals and companies that do securities business in Utah must have a license. You can use this website to see if a business has been licensed.


Search for licensees with the Division of Real Estate
Appraisal agents, real estate agents, and real estate companies must be licensed and registered with the state. This search will tell you if a particular agent or company has been licensed.


Search for licensees with the Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing (DOPL)
DOPL licenses over 60 types of professionals, such as nurses, barbers, or plumbers. If someone has been licensed by DOPL, this means they meet some minimum standard for their profession. You can use this search to see if a professional you interacted with has been licensed.


Search for actions taken by the Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing (DOPL)
DOPL investigates and disciplines professionals that have committed unlawful or unethical acts. This search will tell you if a certain professional has been disciplined by DOPL in the past.
